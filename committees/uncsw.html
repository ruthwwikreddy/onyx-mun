The Committee

The United Nations Commission on the Status of Women (UNCSW) is the principal global intergovernmental body exclusively dedicated to the promotion of gender equality and the empowerment of women. Established in 1946, the UNCSW plays a crucial role in promoting women's rights, documenting the reality of women's lives throughout the world, and shaping global standards on gender equality and the empowerment of women.

At ONYX MUN 2026, the UNCSW will focus on contemporary challenges to gender equality, including economic empowerment, political participation, and the protection of women's rights in digital spaces. Delegates will work towards comprehensive resolutions that address systemic barriers and promote inclusive development.

Discussion Framework

  • Economic empowerment of women in the evolving global labor market.
  • Enhancing women's participation and leadership in political decision-making.
  • Addressing gender-based violence and harassment in digital and physical spaces.
  • Promoting access to quality education and vocational training for girls and women.
  • Ensuring gender-responsive policies in climate action and sustainable development.

Expectations

Delegates in the UNCSW are expected to advocate for progressive and inclusive policies that reflect the diversity of women's experiences globally. Effective negotiation and a commitment to advancing the global agenda for gender equality are essential.

Council Brief

UNCSW is the primary global forum for advancing the rights and empowerment of women and girls everywhere.
